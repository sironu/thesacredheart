Introduction

History of the Devotion to the Sacred Heart

The devotion to the Sacred Heart of Jesus has its roots in early Christian mysticism, particularly in the visions of Saint Gertrude the Great in the 13th century and Saint Margaret Mary Alacoque in the 17th century. However, it was Saint Margaret Mary's visions that significantly shaped the modern devotion.

In 1673-1675, Saint Margaret Mary reported visions of Jesus Christ, in which he revealed his Sacred Heart, symbolizing his divine love for humanity. She shared these experiences with her confessor, Saint Claude de la Colombière. The devotion gained momentum, and in 1765, Pope Clement XIII approved the Feast of the Sacred Heart.

The devotion emphasizes Jesus' love, compassion, and sacrifice. It encourages trust, devotion, and reparation to his Sacred Heart, often depicted in art as a heart surrounded by thorns and flames. The feast day is celebrated on the Friday after the Corpus Christi octave.

Throughout history, numerous saints, popes, and devotees have promoted the devotion, which has become a cornerstone of Catholic spirituality, focusing on the transformative power of Jesus' love.

    Leader: - Prayer to the sacred heart at the beginning of the Adoration

    All: -

    Behold me, once again prostrate at your feet. O Divine Savior, as a faithful adorer of your Sacred Heart, and as a consoling friend who comes to make reparation for the outrages which this Heart Incessantly receives. At last the precious moment has arrived when, according to your desire, I am nearer Thee. I know that you are ready to welcome me and to listen to me. Your own voice has called me. My heart was longing to hear this call; and though I know my unworthiness, docile to your summons, I come without fear. And yet who am I, O my God, that I should fulfill on earth the sublime office of the angels and saints to adore you, saying with them from the depths of my heart: “Holy Holy Holy is the heart of Jesus”. I adore you, heart of Jesus, united substantially with the word of God. I love you, heart of Jesus, and symbol of my Saviour’s love for me. I honour you, heart of Jesus, the most beautiful, the best, and the sweetest of all hearts. I dedicate myself to adore, love and honour you during all my life here on earth, so that I may glorify you throughout eternity.

    Who am I, O my divine savour that I should dare to make reparation to you; that I should ask you to accept me as your victim. Even though I surrender myself forever to you and concentrate myself to you unreservedly to your service, is my homage of reparation pleasing to you? I have so many personal amends to make! Will not my own sins be a hindrance to your divine mercy? Behold me prostrate in your present with sentiments of deep humility and loving compassion. Look upon me with tenderness from your sanctuary where you dost open your heart to me. Forgive my unworthiness, Divine Jesus and grant me that grace to spend this hour in a manner pleasing to you, so that your heart may be consoled and may receive reparation for the forgetfulness and coldness of those who abandon you, and for the profanation of those who outrage you even in the very sacrament of your love. The thought of their outrages fill my soul with horror, while the remembrance of my own sins overwhelms me with grief. Pardon, O lord, pardon my infidelities and those of all poor sinners. I acknowledge you as the God of my heart. May I never forget you; may I never insult you again. Would that I could, even at the cost of my life, repair and make you forget so much ingratitude! O dearest Jesus enlighten the blind who slight you, and convert sinners who abuse your benefits and wound your most sacred heart. Accept as a token of reparation my avowal, my sorrow, my gratitude and my promises. Avowal of my sins which I detest, sorrow for the outrages which grieve you; gratitude for your merciful kindness, finally the desire, the will, and the sincere resolution to make reparation by adoring you perpetually upon earth, so that I may adore you forever in heaven where I shall love you without fear of losing you, O God of my heart. Amen. May the sacred heart of Jesus be loved, praised, blessed and adored forever and ever! Amen.
