History of the Devotion to the Sacred Heart

The devotion to the Sacred Heart of Jesus has its roots in early Christian mysticism, particularly in the visions of Saint Gertrude the Great in the 13th century and Saint Margaret Mary Alacoque in the 17th century. However, it was Saint Margaret Mary's visions that significantly shaped the modern devotion.

In 1673-1675, Saint Margaret Mary reported visions of Jesus Christ, in which he revealed his Sacred Heart, symbolizing his divine love for humanity. She shared these experiences with her confessor, Saint Claude de la Colombi√®re. The devotion gained momentum, and in 1765, Pope Clement XIII approved the Feast of the Sacred Heart.

The devotion emphasizes Jesus' love, compassion, and sacrifice. It encourages trust, devotion, and reparation to his Sacred Heart, often depicted in art as a heart surrounded by thorns and flames. The feast day is celebrated on the Friday after the Corpus Christi octave.

Throughout history, numerous saints, popes, and devotees have promoted the devotion, which has become a cornerstone of Catholic spirituality, focusing on the transformative power of Jesus' love.
